Biographic Books – Real Lives, Real Lessons

Biographic books offer a powerful window into the lives of remarkable individuals who shaped history, challenged norms, and inspired the world. From scientists to social leaders, each story captures struggles, breakthroughs, and personal growth. Whether it's a journey of resilience, sacrifice, or transformation, biographies let us learn from real experiences and see the human side behind greatness.


Wings of Fire – A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

  • An inspiring autobiography of India’s Missile Man and former President.
  • It details his humble beginnings, scientific journey, and deep patriotism.

The Diary of a Young Girl – Anne Frank

  • The emotional diary of a Jewish girl hiding from the Nazis during WWII.
  • A heartbreaking but powerful view of courage, hope, and humanity.

Long Walk to Freedom – Nelson Mandela

  • A gripping story of Mandela’s fight against apartheid, years in prison, and rise as South Africa’s leader and symbol of peace.

Steve Jobs – Walter Isaacson

  • The official biography of Apple’s visionary founder.
  • It explores his creative genius, flaws, and revolutionary impact on technology.

I Am Malala – Malala Yousafzai

  • The story of a young girl who stood up to the Taliban for her right to education and became the youngest Nobel Prize winner.

Becoming – Michelle Obama

  • A deeply personal account of the former First Lady's life from her roots to her role in the White House, filled with hope and empowerment.
